Inventions
In 1843, Ada Lovelace's partner Babbage was developing the Analytical Engine, a more complicated version of the Difference Engine. He asked Lovelace to translate French text from his engineer into English. Lovelace not only translated the notes but added her own, signing them “A.A.L.”
Bernoulli numbers are a sequence of numbers used in advanced math. Created by Ada Lovelace, They help solve problems involving fractions and sharing things. Think of it like this: imagine you have a cake, and you want to divide it among your friends, but the pieces aren't all the same size, so there are some crumbs left over. Bernoulli numbers help you find out how many crumbs you'll have when you divide the cake among different numbers of friends
